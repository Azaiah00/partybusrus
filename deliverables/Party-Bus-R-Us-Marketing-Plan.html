THE GOAL

North star: booked revenue

Not leads. Not clicks. Not impressions. Not even bookings. Booked revenue from partybusrus.com per month — because that's the only number that pays the buses. Everything below ladders up to it.

The economics that make this work: Average DMV party bus booking value is $900–1,800. At a 15% lead-to-booking rate and ~$40 cost per lead, customer acquisition cost lands around $260 — well inside the unit economics. Month 1 is roughly break-even by design (we pay to learn). Month 3 is the first month of meaningful profit.
01 · THE FIELD

Market & positioning

Eight DMV competitors audited. Pricing runs $150–$350/hr with a 15–30% weekend premium. Only one competitor publishes pricing. Here's where we take share.

The 3 most dangerous competitors

Threat #1

The DMV Party Bus

16K Instagram, transparent $10/person/hr pricing in bio, IG-native lead capture. Owns the search bar and the social bar at once.

Threat #2

DC 4 Party

VIP / red-carpet positioning that overlaps our higher-end party voice. Thin social proof but aggressive ad spend — could undercut our paid CPCs.

Threat #3

Uptown Bus

Broadest service taxonomy in the market (prom → corporate). Solid Google Business signal. Hardest to outrank on generic terms.

Defensive plays: (1) Bid on these 3 brand names as competitor-conquest keywords. (2) Beat The DMV Party Bus on transparency — publish a real quote calculator, not just a per-person number. (3) Beat DC 4 Party on social proof — 30 reviews in 90 days. (4) Beat Uptown Bus on niche depth — we win bachelorette while they spread thin.

Universal gaps we can exploit

Video

Not one competitor has embedded fleet video. We have 8 clips already shot — embed them and instantly differentiate.

Spanish content

Zero competitors offer Spanish. 17% of the DMV is Hispanic — a market nobody is fighting for.

Quinceañera

No competitor has a Quinceañera landing page. A $20K+/year segment that's wide open.

Instant quote calculator

Zero competitors offer instant pricing. A 4-input calculator (passengers, hours, date, ZIP) removes the form-friction wall.

Schema markup

Weak across all 8. Full LocalBusiness, Service, FAQ, and Vehicle schema vaults us into AI Overviews.

Loyalty / planners

No competitor rewards repeat customers. Wedding planners book 8–15 events/year — lock them in.

Positioning recommendation: Lead with bachelorette + birthday (emotional pull, highest social-share rate, easiest to advertise on Meta/IG). Layer transparent pricing as the operational moat (only DMV operator with a real quote calculator). Seed wine tours as the Q2/Q3 vertical (high AOV, daytime utilization, repeat customers via planners).
02 · POSITIONING

Two front doors, one business

Run partylimobusdc.com (premium) and partybusrus.com (party) as separate, attributable sites. Same fleet, same dispatch, twice the search real estate and clean ad attribution — a proven white-hat strategy DMV limo competitors already use.

partylimobusdc.com — "The Premium Door"

Audience: weddings, corporate transport, executive shuttles, milestone birthdays (40+), funerals, anniversaries.
Voice: "DMV's premier coachway service." Polished, refined, trust-building.
SEO focus: limo service DC, wedding transportation Virginia, corporate shuttle Maryland.
Lead magnets: wedding transportation guide, corporate account application.

partybusrus.com — "The Party Door"

Audience: bachelor/bachelorette, 21st & 30th birthdays, prom, winery tours, brewery crawls, nightlife groups.
Voice: "Roll deep. The party starts the second you board." Energetic, fun, no-pretense.
SEO focus: party bus DC, bachelorette bus DMV, winery tour bus Virginia, birthday bus.
Lead magnets: bachelorette route planner, brewery crawl itineraries, group cost-splitter.

What stays shared (non-negotiable)

  • One Google Business Profile. One physical business = one GBP. Two profiles for the same address violates Google policy and gets one suspended.
  • Reviews. All reviews live on the single GBP; both sites display the same embedded feed.
  • Fleet + dispatch + pricing. Same buses, same calendar (no double-booking), same rates — just presented to a different audience.
Why it works: Both sites can rank for "party bus DC" — but partybusrus.com ranks stronger because the domain matches the query. We stop competing with ourselves and start covering both ends of the market. 8-week rollout.
03 · FIX THE LEAKS

Foundation: stop losing leads now

The new site looks beautiful — and that's the problem. SEO + go-to-market readiness scored 28/100. Three blockers are quietly killing results. None are hard to fix.

Blocker #1

SPA hash routing

Single-file site with #/page URLs. Google indexes only the home page — 45 of 46 pages are invisible. Re-architect to a multi-page static site (Astro). Highest-ROI move of 2026.

Blocker #2

Quote form is broken

Form fields lack name attributes and there's no backend. Every inquiry submitted right now is lost. Wire to Formspree / Netlify Forms this week.

Blocker #3

Zero analytics

No GA4, GTM, Meta Pixel, or call tracking. We're flying blind. Install all four in week 1 — we can't improve what we can't measure.

The 15 quick wins — week 1

Tracking

Install GA4 + GTM · Meta Pixel via GTM · CallRail number + swap site phone · wire phone-click events to GA4.

Site fixes

Replace 1.1 MB favicon · remove duplicate testimonial · fix Google review URL · strip insurance/credentials & "6 years" leftovers · fix About-page pricing contradiction.

Performance & schema

Compress 3 oversized hero images to <300 KB · add name attributes + wire form backend · add lang/hreflang to Spanish pages · make JSON-LD URLs absolute.

Strengths we keep: a strong 2,438-word home page (most competitors have 400–800), genuine city pages, alt text on every image, LocalBusiness schema foundation, and a locked-in brand voice that can't be copied.

10 · THE PATH

90-day roadmap

Week-by-week milestones, with decision gates and kill criteria so we don't burn budget on losers.

Week 1

Tracking foundation + critical fixes

GA4 / GTM / Meta Pixel / CallRail installed. Form wired to a backend. Favicon, duplicate testimonial, broken review URL fixed. Zero leads lost from here on.

Week 2

Schema + GBP + content gaps

Schema enriched. GBP optimized with 30+ photos and weekly posts. 9 blog posts get real photos. Fleet detail pages spec'd for dev.

Week 3

IG content batch + citations

10 reels + 4 carousels scheduled. 30 citations submitted. Highlights structure live. DM templates in use.

Week 4

Google Ads pilot + partnerships

$80/day Google Ads live. First Meta campaign warming. 18 partnership emails sent. Gate (day 14): if CPL > $80, pause Competitor Conquest.

Weeks 5–6

Multi-page site re-architecture begins

SPA → multi-page static site (Astro). Every city, occasion, and fleet vehicle becomes its own indexable URL. The single biggest ROI move of 2026.

Weeks 7–8

Meta scale + IG hits 1,500

Meta to $50/day, Google to $160/day on winners. Gate: kill any ad group with CPL > 2× account average.

Weeks 9–10

New site live · Spanish parity

Multi-page rebuild deployed, 301 redirects mapped, new sitemap fed to Search Console. Spanish version built to feature parity.

Weeks 11–12

TikTok launch + scale-up

TikTok Spark Ads on best IG content. Google to $250/day, Meta to $80/day. Gate: if combined ROAS < 2.5× over 21 rolling days, freeze scale and audit landing pages.

Day 90

Review & reset

Full review with Robert — channel mix, ROAS, lead-to-booking rate, IG followers, ranking deltas. Decide month-4 shape: scale or hold-and-optimize.

Kill criteria: Google ad group CPL > 2× account average for 14 days = pause. Meta ad set CPL > $35 for 7 days = pause. Any channel ROAS < 1.5× over 21 rolling days = stop, audit, restart with fixed creative or landing page.
