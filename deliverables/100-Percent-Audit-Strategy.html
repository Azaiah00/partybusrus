01 · The Score

Where we stand today

The new partybusrus.com site looks beautiful — and that's exactly the problem. Underneath the surface, Google sees almost none of it. Here's the unvarnished number.

28
/ 100

SEO + go-to-market readiness

We have a 2,438-word home page with strong on-brand copy, real photography, and a polished design. But the site is a single-file SPA with hash routing — meaning 45 of 46 pages are invisible to Google. The quote form is non-functional, zero analytics are installed, and the favicon is a 1.1 MB PNG that browsers refuse to render.

The good news: none of these are hard to fix. Most are 1-4 hours of work. The biggest one (re-architecting from SPA to multi-page) is the highest-ROI move we will make in 2026.

The 5 strengths we keep

★

Strong home page copy

2,438 words of on-brand, on-voice copy. Most competitors have 400-800 words of generic boilerplate. This is a real moat.

◎

LocalBusiness schema exists

JSON-LD is present — needs a few additions (review aggregate, services, areaServed) but the foundation is laid.

✔

Unique city pages

Each DC/MD/VA city page is genuinely written, not templated. Once they're crawlable, they'll rank.

☀

Alt text on every image

Often skipped on premium sites. Ours is clean — accessibility and SEO both benefit.

☺

Brand voice locked in

Energetic, DMV-rooted, no-corporate-fluff. That's worth more than any technical fix because it can't be copied.

The 5 critical blockers

⚠

SPA hash routing

Single-file site with #/page URLs. Google indexes only the home page — 45 other pages are invisible. BLOCKER #1.

⚠

Quote form is broken

Form fields lack name attributes and have no backend. Every inquiry submitted right now is lost. Every. One.

⚠

Zero analytics

No GA4, no GTM, no Meta Pixel, no call tracking. We're flying blind. Cannot improve what we cannot measure.

⚠

46 H1 tags in one document

Every "page" is technically a section inside the SPA, and each has an H1. To Google this reads as a chaotic, unfocused document.

⚠

1.1 MB PNG as favicon

The favicon is the full brand lockup at 1.1 MB. Most browsers refuse to render it. Tab shows blank. Looks unprofessional.

Bottom line

We have a beautiful site that Google mostly can't see, the form leaks every lead, and we have zero tracking. Fix these three things and we 5x our results overnight. None of these are huge projects — the SPA fix is the biggest and it's 2-3 weeks of work for a measurable doubling of indexed pages.

04 · The Algorithm

SEO readiness for 2026

Local SEO in 2026 looks different from 2024. AI Overviews now trigger on 68% of local queries. Here's what Google weighs — and what we have versus what we need.

2026 local SEO signal weights

Google Business Profile (proximity, reviews, photos, categories, posts)32%
On-page SEO (titles, H1s, content depth, schema, internal links)19%
Reviews (count, velocity, rating, response rate, keywords in reviews)16%
Backlinks (domain authority, local citations, brand mentions)15%
Behavioral & other (CTR, dwell time, mobile UX, Core Web Vitals)18%
What changed in 2026

AI Overviews trigger on 68% of local queries — versus only 39% triggering a traditional local pack. Google's AI synthesizes business info from schema, GBP, and a small handful of high-authority pages. Schema markup is now the single most underleveraged ranking factor because it's how AI Overviews quote you.

What we have vs. what we need

✔ What we have

  • LocalBusiness JSON-LD on the home page (partial, needs additions)
  • Strong on-brand copy — 2,438 words on the home page
  • Unique city pages for DC, MD, VA
  • Alt text on every visible image
  • HTTPS + mobile responsive
  • Real photography beating most competitors

⚠ What we need

  • Multi-page architecture so each "page" is actually crawlable
  • Sitemap.xml submitted to Search Console
  • Canonical URLs on every page
  • FAQ schema on top 6 service pages (boosts AI Overview citations)
  • Service schema for each occasion (bachelorette, wedding, prom)
  • Vehicle schema on each fleet detail page
  • BreadcrumbList schema site-wide

Tool decisions worth making in week 1

Moovs vs Limo Anywhere Which booking system?+

Moovs is newer, more modern UI, integrates with Stripe out of the box, and has a better online-booking widget for the public website. Limo Anywhere is the legacy DMV standard — most other operators use it, which matters if we want to take overflow work from other companies. Recommendation: start with Moovs unless Robert already has Limo Anywhere accounts. The modern booking UX matters more than dispatch network integration at this stage.

Klaviyo vs Mailchimp Which email platform?+

Klaviyo every time. Mailchimp lost the e-commerce/automation arms race years ago. Klaviyo's free tier handles 250 contacts (we'll cross that in month 2). After that, it scales to ~$45/mo and the segmentation + automation features pay for themselves on a single email sequence.

Formspree vs Netlify Forms vs HubSpot Form backend?+

If we move to Netlify Pro hosting, Netlify Forms is the simplest answer (built-in, $0 extra at our volume). If we host elsewhere, Formspree at $10/mo is the cleanest. HubSpot Forms are free and connect to a CRM but the embed code is bulky and slows page load. My pick: Netlify Forms if we go Netlify Pro, Formspree otherwise.

First-month tool-onboarding order

Day 1: GA4 + GTM + Meta Pixel. Day 2: CallRail. Day 3: Formspree (or Netlify Forms). Week 2: Klaviyo + HoneyBook. Week 3: Moovs booking integration. Week 4: Looker Studio dashboard. Everything else is optional polish.
