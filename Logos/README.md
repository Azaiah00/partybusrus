## Why these are the top picks

**The script "R Us" breakthrough (Logos 12 & 13):**
The calligraphic italic treatment on "R Us" transforms what was a kitschy Toys-R-Us reference into a deliberate, elegant calligraphic flourish. You keep the brand name recognition but make "R Us" feel premium instead of accidental. This is the single most important design move in the whole set.

**The all-gold crown monogram (Logo 15):**
Most refined of the monograms. The interlocked PBR letterforms feel like a true monogram, not just letters in a circle. The crown signals premium without being cartoonish. Works at any size — favicon to billboard.

---

## ⚠️ The "PBR" issue to address in Round 2

The monogram letters "PBR" can read as **Pabst Blue Ribbon** (the beer brand). The interlocked letterforms soften it, but at a glance some people will see the beer logo. In Round 2, we'll test:
- **"PB"** monogram (Party Bus — cleaner, no beer association)
- **"P"** alone with crown (most minimalist)
- **"PBR"** interlocked (kept for reference)

The refinement prompt I gave you covers all three so you can pick the winner.
