4. Regulatory / licensing — what you must have and surface publicly

This is missing entirely from the earlier audit but is essential for wedding and corporate buyers. The DMV regulatory picture for a party bus operator:

  • WMATC operating authority (Washington Metropolitan Area Transit Commission, wmatc.gov) is required to carry paying passengers point-to-point within DC + Montgomery/Prince George's MD + Arlington/Fairfax/Alexandria/Falls Church VA. Surface your WMATC number on the website.
  • USDOT number + MC (Motor Carrier) authority from FMCSA for interstate trips (e.g., DC↔Baltimore). For 16+ passenger vehicles, the federal minimum is $5 million passenger liability insurance.
  • Virginia DMV passenger carrier registration (Form OA-150) + $25K surety bond for intrastate VA work outside the WMATC zone.
  • Maryland PSC Transportation Division authority for intrastate MD work outside WMATC.
  • DC DFHV (Department of For-Hire Vehicles) for DC pickups; Special Event Permits ($250/vehicle) during authorized DC events.
  • Alcohol: Virginia does not issue mobile liquor licenses → party buses in VA are BYOB only, and the operator's role is driver + ID-check. DC requires an ABRA temporary license to serve alcohol; out-of-state licenses are not honored. Maryland is BYOB-tolerant but you cannot sell or serve.
